Voice & Tone

How we communicate with words is just as important as how we communicate with visuals.

Voice

Voice is important because when you speak and write, the audience should understand your personality and the culture and personality of Iodine

Tone

Tone is a subset of voice. Tone is all about the mood or attitude of communication. It makes sense: the same personality (voice) can have different attitudes (tone) in different contexts. As with voice, tone should be appropriate to the goal, purpose and audience - but tone can vary a bit depending on the when and the where.

Be positive and explain why

Always begin a communication with positivity and optimism.
For example:
“We don’t have a feature that does that."
versus
“We’re constantly looking for feedback and suggestions... but we don’t currently have a feature like that...”

Be professional

Grammatical errors should be avoided. Proof twice, publish once.
Ask a peer to review what you've written before you send it.

Be warm and converstaional

Much of our communication should reflect our culture, which leans more toward personal than formal.

Using contractions is fine (you’re welcome vs. you are welcome)
Using personal language is encouraged (Hi! vs. Hello.)

Be clear and concise

People tend to glaze over when they have to read too much. Keep it short, sweet and to the point.

What NOT to say

  • Algorithm
  • Software calculation
  • Predict (Iodine prioritizes; it doesn't predict!)
  • Machine Learning
  • Mapping
  • Trgger
  • Heuristics
  • Users
  • Suggested Query (Iodine suggest potential conditions, not queries)

The CDI specialists are the clinical experts

Always remember that Iodine only prioritizing patients, conditions, and opportunities. CDI specialists should always be thought of as the experts who use their best clinical judgement in reviewing a case.

Typography

The main typeface that is used throughout Iodine's collateral is Rubik. You can download it here, open the zip file and save Rubik-Regular.tff into your Font Book to use in your documents.
Download Font


Use for web
Rubik is hosted by google and can be implemented to your site:

To embed Rubik into a webpage, copy this code into the <head> of your HTML document: 

<link href="https://fonts.googleapis.com/css?family=Rubik:300,400,500,700" rel="stylesheet">

Use the following CSS rules to specify the Rubik family: 

body  {
font-family: 'Rubik', Helvetica Neue, HelveticaNeue, Helvetica, sans-serif;
}

Elevations & Shadows

In general, don't use drop-shadows.

When and how to use shadows

Use drop-shadows subtly and sparingling. They easily make work look super dated so learn to use them well.

Drop shadows are tempting but can easily distract from and clutter the designs.

Use drop-shadows to subtly help text pop from their background.

Long shadows have a more modern feel. They work well with the flat icon, minimalist look. These long shadows add an interesting, yet simple design element that doesn’t distract from the main image.

When and how to Elevation

In general we use three elvations that float above the base.

Elavation 1: The first layer that sits on-top of the base layer
Elavation 2: Any element that feels like it rests above elements at elevation 1
Elavation 3: We use this elavation as the hover state for elements in Elevation 1 and 2 and and the drop shadow for modal windows

Elevation 1
box-shadow: 0 1px 2px 0 rgba(36, 50, 66, 0.15);

Elevation 2
box-shadow: 0 3px 5px 0 rgba(36, 50, 66, 0.2);

Elevation 3
box-shadow: 0 20px 30px 0 rgba(35, 50, 66, 0.14);
